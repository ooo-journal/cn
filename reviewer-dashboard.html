Abstract

This study explores the theoretical thermodynamic properties of cat purring and its potential applications in interstellar propulsion systems. We hypothesize that the unique frequency patterns of feline purring could be harnessed to create a novel form of propulsion that defies current physical limitations. Through computational modeling and theoretical analysis, we demonstrate that the vibrational energy produced by cat purring contains quantum properties that could be amplified to generate propulsion forces sufficient for interstellar travel. This research opens new avenues for space exploration and challenges conventional physics paradigms.
